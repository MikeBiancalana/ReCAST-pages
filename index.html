Monthly Loan Payment (Amortization)

The standard fixed-payment amortization formula, used for both the existing loan and the new construction loan. Given a principal P, a monthly interest rate r (annual rate ÷ 12), and a term of n months:

payment = P × r × (1 + r)ⁿ / ((1 + r)ⁿ − 1) If r = 0 (zero-interest): payment = P / n

When an explicit monthly payment is entered for the existing loan, that amount is used directly each month so long as it exceeds the accrued interest. Any surplus above interest reduces principal. If the explicit payment is less than required interest, the simulation falls back to the amortized minimum.

Loan Balance Month-by-Month

Each month the outstanding balance is updated by accruing interest and subtracting the payment:

balance(m+1) = balance(m) × (1 + r) − payment

The simulation iterates this every month from today through the full projection horizon. When balance falls to zero or below, the loan is marked paid. If an early lump-sum payoff is triggered, the full remaining balance is subtracted from cash reserves in that month.

Interest Rate Adjustment

The existing loan carries the current rate until the rate-change date, then switches to the adjusted rate for all subsequent months. No refinancing or prepayment penalty is modeled at the transition — only the rate changes. The monthly payment is recalculated each month after the switch based on remaining balance and remaining term:

remaining_term = months from current date to payoff date payment_after_adj = monthlyPayment(balance, adjustedRate, remaining_term)

Construction Cost Inflation

Construction costs are assumed to grow at a constant annual compound rate from today to the new loan date. The inflated cost at loan time is:

inflated_cost = today_cost × (1 + inflation_rate)^years_delay years_delay = (new_loan_date − today) / 365.25

This is a simplifying assumption. Real construction inflation is volatile and project-specific. The input field lets you test a range of assumptions.

Down Payment & New Loan Principal

When "Apply freed cash toward down payment" is enabled, the model uses up to 80% of cash on hand at the new loan date as a down payment, capped at 30% of the inflated construction cost. The remainder becomes the new loan principal:

down_payment = min(cash × 0.8, inflated_cost × 0.3) new_loan_principal = inflated_cost − down_payment

The 80% and 30% caps preserve a cash buffer and avoid over-leveraging the down payment. They can be thought of as conservative placeholders — the tool is most useful for relative comparisons between scenarios.

Net Interest Savings (Early Payoff)

To estimate net savings from paying off early, the model computes the interest that would have accrued between the early payoff date and the scheduled payoff date — at the post-adjustment rate — then subtracts an opportunity cost:

gross_savings = Σ interest(m) for m in [early_payoff → payoff] (at adjusted rate, on the balance at early payoff) opportunity_cost = payoff_balance × 4% × months / 12 net_savings = gross_savings − opportunity_cost

The 4% opportunity cost approximates a conservative savings/money-market yield. A higher-yielding alternative (e.g., bonds, equities) would reduce net savings further. This is a first-order estimate.

Cash Flow Projection

Each month, cash changes by:

Δcash = giving − operating_expenses − existing_loan_payment − new_loan_payment (once active)

This is a net cash model — it does not distinguish between an operating fund and a capital reserve. All inflows and outflows are pooled. The minimum cash value reported is the lowest point this running balance reaches across the entire projection.

Assumptions & Limitations

This tool is a planning aid, not a certified financial model. Key simplifications:

• Giving and expenses are held constant (no growth or seasonal variation)
• No prepayment penalties on the existing loan
• New loan is assumed fully drawn at origination
• No investment return modeled on reserves beyond the 4% opportunity cost
• Tax treatment of interest is not considered
• All dollars are nominal (not inflation-adjusted for purchasing power)

Results should be reviewed alongside a qualified financial advisor before making binding commitments.
